Kate's Kollectibles

This is a mock-up of I website I am building to showcase antiques and collectibles I'm helping my mom sell. The genesis of the idea originates from a trip my sister and I made to an antique store with three boxes of collectibles we couldn't identify on the web. The store itself contained several dozen booths from individual sellers (an "antique mall"). A handful of the vendors were there, and they excitedly looked over the items, told us about the items' histories, and gave us a fair offer for what they wanted. It was a good experience, and I could tell they were passionate about what they were doing and believed in being ethical with their offers. We walked out with one box left and over $60.

When we were leaving, I talked to the main proprietor about the remaining spread of antiques on Mom's dining room table. I told her I would email her pictures of everything so she and her fellow vendors could look at them and tell us what they were interested in. When I finished the photoshoot, I realized I had too many pictures for an email and I needed to create something more structured. Kate's Kollectibles was born.

The website is not intended to be a "store" per se, rather a way to showcase each item with a description and photos so collectors can review what she has available. Some items will be for sale, in which case they'll contact me to make an offer. For other items, I'll be seeking their advice to identify the manufacturer and name of the item. I'll probably use a different set of colors for these.

I built this page from scratch in VSCode, using the W3Schools tutorials for guidance for Bootstrap. I liked the appearance of the Bootstrap panels for presenting the items, so I built the structure on that. For the photos, I knew I wanted a side-by-side presentation. I found the "responsive" dynamic image resizing distracting, so I opted for a fixed thumbnail format. The items have a variable number of photos, so I used a format that doesn't rely on Bootstrap columns but still allows photos to scroll to the next line on smaller screens.
